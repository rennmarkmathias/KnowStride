7. Cossack Hetmanate — Leadership and Military Tradition

In the middle of the 1600s, a new political order appeared on the steppe and forest-steppe borderlands of Eastern Europe: the Cossack Hetmanate. It emerged from a society shaped by river crossings, raids, seasonal campaigns, and the practical demands of defending scattered settlements.

Leadership in this environment was never simply “civil” or “military”. The same figures who negotiated with neighboring powers also raised regiments, managed supply lines, and enforced discipline.
